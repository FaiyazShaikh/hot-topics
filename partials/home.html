The Value of Money

Money has transitioned from a commodity-backed system to a digital abstraction. Historically, value was tied to physical assets like gold, but today, money is primarily a social contract backed by institutional trust.

My Analysis: The rapid rise of decentralized finance suggests that the "value" of money is shifting toward mathematical transparency. While this democratizes finance, it introduces volatility. As noted by the International Monetary Fund, digital currencies may improve efficiency, but they require robust regulatory frameworks to remain stable (IMF, 2024).

GMO Food: A Scientific Necessity?

Genetically Modified Organisms (GMOs) are designed to withstand pests and survive extreme weather. Despite their benefits, they remain a point of ethical and environmental debate.

My Analysis: GMOs are a double-edged sword. They are essential for food security as our population grows, but the monopolization of seed patents poses a risk to biodiversity. Scientific consensus from the World Health Organization maintains that currently available GMO foods are safe for consumption, but ongoing monitoring is vital (WHO, 2023).

The View of World in 2100

Predictions for 2100 focus on two extremes: climate catastrophe or a technological utopia. The reality will likely be a hybrid of AI integration and hyper-sustainable design.

My Analysis: By 2100, the definition of "humanity" will likely include deep integration with AI and biotechnology. If current trends in renewable energy continue, our cities may become self-sustaining ecosystems. However, the success of this vision depends entirely on the policy decisions made in the current decade regarding carbon emissions and ethical AI governance.
